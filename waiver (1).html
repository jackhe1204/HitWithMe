Liability & Risk — Waiver of Liability for Personal Injury

Statutory Risk Warning — NSW Civil Liability Act 2002

Under the Civil Liability Act 2002 (NSW), Division 5 — Recreational Activities, this is a formal warning that tennis coaching involves recreational activity that carries inherent and obvious risks of personal injury. By proceeding, you are taken to be aware of these risks. This warning is given to you prior to your engagement in the activity, as required by Section 5M of the Act.

Obvious and inherent risks of tennis coaching include but are not limited to:

  • Muscle strains, sprains, and tears from physical exertion
  • Joint injuries including ankles, knees, elbows, and shoulders
  • Trips and falls on court surfaces
  • Being struck by a tennis ball or racket
  • Heat-related illness during outdoor sessions
  • Aggravation of pre-existing medical conditions or injuries
  • General physical fatigue and overexertion

Waiver of liability — personal injury only (pursuant to ACL s.139A):

In accordance with the Australian Consumer Law, Section 139A and the Civil Liability Act 2002 (NSW), Section 5N, and to the extent permitted by law, you release and discharge the coach from any liability for personal injury sustained during or arising from tennis coaching sessions, including injury resulting from the coach's failure to exercise reasonable care and skill, except where caused by the coach's reckless or intentional conduct.

Note: This waiver applies to personal injury only, as required by law. It does not purport to exclude liability for property damage, economic loss or any non-excludable consumer guarantee.

Indemnity: You agree to indemnify and hold harmless the coach from any claim, cost or expense arising from your participation in coaching sessions, to the full extent permitted by NSW law.

Session Safety & Emergency Procedure

Jack commits to the following safety standards at every session:

  • Verbal warm-up and safety briefing at the start of each session
  • Assessing client physical readiness before commencing drills
  • Limiting sessions to appropriate skill and intensity level
  • Ceasing activity immediately if a client reports pain or discomfort
  • Directing clients to seek professional medical attention if any injury occurs

In an emergency: the session will be stopped immediately, 000 called if serious, and your nominated emergency contact notified as soon as possible. The coach will not attempt to treat or move an injured client — professional medical assistance will be awaited.
